A Tribute to ANC

Description of the ANC Logo

The African National Congress (ANC) has a rich history, marked by the leadership of remarkable individuals who have guided the organization through pivotal moments in South Africa's journey. Here is a timeline of some notable ANC presidents up until my knowledge cutoff date in September 2021:

  1. 1912 - 1917: John Dube - John Dube was the founding president of the ANC and played a crucial role in its early years.
  2. 1917 - 1924: S. R. M. (Solomon) Plaatje - Solomon Plaatje succeeded John Dube and worked to raise awareness of racial injustices in South Africa.
  3. 1940 - 1949: Alfred B. Xuma - Alfred Xuma led the ANC during World War II and focused on civil rights for Black South Africans.
  4. 1949 - 1952: Dr. James Moroka - Dr. James Moroka was a medical doctor and led the ANC during a pivotal period of resistance against apartheid policies.
  5. 1952 - 1967: Albertina Sisulu (Acting President) - Albertina Sisulu served as acting president during a critical time and was a prominent anti-apartheid activist.
  6. 1967 - 1991: Oliver Tambo - Oliver Tambo is one of the most iconic ANC presidents, leading the organization during its years in exile and advocating for international pressure against apartheid.
  7. 1991 - 1997: Nelson Mandela - Nelson Mandela became the first Black president of South Africa and played a central role in the transition to democracy.
  8. 1997 - 2007: Thabo Mbeki - Thabo Mbeki succeeded Mandela and focused on economic growth and development.
  9. 2007 - 2017: Jacob Zuma - Jacob Zuma served as ANC president and later as the president of South Africa, facing significant controversy during his tenure.
  10. 2017 - 2022: Cyril Ramaphosa (as of my last knowledge update) - Cyril Ramaphosa became ANC president and later the president of South Africa, with a focus on anti-corruption and economic reforms.
